The Colorado Avalanche are the most dominant team in hockey, but they have a major weakness

hockey
nhl
colorado
Author

Jason Balderaz

Published

April 10, 2026

The Colorado Avalanche are having a historic season. They are the first team in the league to reach 50 wins and have already clinched the #1 seed in the Western Conference and have won the Presidents’ Trophy. But how dominant have they truly been?

There is no doubt that the Avalanche are the best team in the NHL right now. They lead the league in most goals scored at 287, least goals against at 191 and lead the league in penalty kill percentage at 81.5%

That means Colorado has consistently had the #1 offense and #1 defense all season, and it’s thanks to their team dynamic.

The big three of Nathan MacKinnon, Cale Makar and Martin Nečas are all top-30 in the league in points, tied for the most players in the top-30 with the Edmonton Oilers.

There are many factors to the Avalanche’s success, so let’s take a look at an example of how successful they have been compared to their competition.

Colorado’s goals for this season make up 3.73% of all goals made this season, which doesn’t seem like a lot, but it is the largest share among other NHL teams. This means Colorado’s offensive firepower is unmatched and continues to improve.

The defense has been just as dominant. As said earlier, the Avs lead the league in penalty kill percentage. Led by two-time Norris trophy winner, Cale Makar, alongside Devon Toews and Sam Malinski, but the real money makers are goalie duo, Scott Wedgewood and Mackenzie Blackwood, who together have earned the top spot in the league in goals against.

But with so much going right for Colorado, there is one glaring issue that has plagued them since last year: the power play.

In the 2025 playoffs, Colorado was eliminated in the first round by the Dallas Stars after seven games. The sore thumb in that scenario was that the Avs special teams unit was really, really bad, going 3-for-22 and 13.6% on the power play for the entire series. This directly resulted in power play coach Ray Bennett being fired.

However, it seems that the struggles never ended, even after the hiring of new assistant coach Dave Hakstol. As of April 7, 2026, the Avalanche sit 31st in the NHL for power play success rate.

So, with as historic of a season they are having, will their power play woes really matter? Obviously, it was a heavy factor last season, but how does it stack up against previous Lord Stanley winners?

It’s not uncommon for Stanley Cup winners to have a low power play percentage. In recent years, teams haven’t even cracked the top 10 in net power play percentage. The first Florida Panthers chip had their power play at 17.1%, good enough for 19th in the league. It also goes to show how good that 2022 Colorado team was, having the highest net PP% out of the last 10 winners.

Since after the Olympic break, the Avalanche’s power play has actually improved, spiking to 18.8% since play has resumed. New additions, such as Nazem Kadri, have added extra firepower to the PP 1 unit, and the future still looks bright for the Avs.
